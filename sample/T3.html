Test 3

This chip is used for the lift-off training. We will hence pattern some shapes and test the etching at different times. Then, we will extract the best parameters to use for the final device lift-off process.

Composition before patterning

- Substrate Layer 1 Layer 2
Material Si + SiO2 PMGI PPA
Thickness SiO2 : 200 nm 180 nm 45 nm

Substrate preparation

  • Diced part of a wafer of Si + 200 nm SiO2
  • Cleaned in acetone during 5 minutes
  • Cleaned in isopropanole during 5 minutes
  • Dried with a N2 gun

PMGI spin coating

Role of this layer : Sacrificial layer for the lift-off.
Steps followed for the fabrication :

  • Chip baked at 190 degrees during 5 minutes (for dehydration)
  • Spin coated PMGI SF2s at 1000 rpm (theoretical thickness : 90 nm)
  • Chip baked at 190 degrees during 3 minutes
  • Let it cool down
  • Spin coated PMGI SF2s at 1000 rpm (theoretical thickness : 90 nm)
  • Chip baked at 190 degrees during 3 minutes

Total thickness of the layer : 180 nm
Thickness measured : No

PPA spin coating

Role of this layer : Layer to pattern with t-SPL technique.
Steps followed for the fabrication :

  • Spin coated PPA 3% at 4000 rpm (theoretical thickness : 45 nm)
  • Chip baked at 90 degrees during 3 minutes

Total thickness of the layer : 45 nm
Thickness measured : No

Wet etching

Steps followed to etch the PMGI layer :

  • Sample immersed into a TMAH solution (AZ 726 MIF) during 260 seconds.
  • Sample rinsed into water during 60 seconds.
  • Sample rinsed into IPA during 60 seconds.
  • Dried with the N2 gun.

Optical microscope image of the sample after the wet etching.

Electrode deposition

To fabricate the electrodes onto the holes induced by the wet etching, we proceed like this (we changed a bit the values):

  • Evaporation of 40 nm of Cr
  • Evaporation of 20 nm of Au
Then, we remove the PPA and PMGI layers by lift-off.
  • Sample immersed into a ... solution during around 30 seconds
  • Sonication at a power of 2 W during 1 minute
  • Sonication at a power of 3 W during 1 minute

Optical microscope image of the sample after the evaporation and lift-off.

Discussion

The t-SPL patterns are almost the same, regardless of the target depth. We notice only a little difference in depth between P1 and P3 and almost no difference between P3 and P5. This finding allows us to ensure that the holes go through all the PPA layer (for P3 and P5). That is also why we see almost no difference between the patterns after the wet etching. The results after the evaporation and lift-off are nice. In P2, one hole doesn't appear so we probably don't overetch. Let's see what happen when we increase again the etching time (test 4).
