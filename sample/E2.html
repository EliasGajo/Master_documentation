E2

This chip contains some exfoliated flakes of MoS2. These flakes have been transferred onto a Si + SiO2 substrate without any patterns. Then the aim of this sample (as well as E1, E3, and E4) is to define and isolate the active regions of our 2D material. We will also use the successful active regions to fabricate a plane surface gas sensor.

  • Diced part of a wafer of Si + 200 nm SiO2
  • Cleaned in acetone during 5 minutes
  • Cleaned in isopropanole during 5 minutes
  • Dried with a N2 gun

Interesting regions

Here are the interesting regions of this chip (the regions where we transferred very thin layers of MoS2) :

Region 1

Hydrophilic treatment

The surface where the MoS2 has been transferred became hydrophobe (due to the scotch tape) and doesn't allow us to spin coat a PPA layer on it. Then, we apply the following treatment in order to remove this hydrophobicity.

  • Chip deeped in a remover 1165 solution during 30 minutes
  • Chip deeped in a AZ726MIF solution during 30 minutes
  • Chip rinsed in water
  • Chip rinsed in IPA
  • Chip baked at 110°C during 3 minutes for dehydration

PPA spin coating

Role of this layer : Layer to pattern with t-SPL technique.
Steps followed for the fabrication :

  • Spin coated PPA 5% at 2000 rpm (theoretical thickness : 100 nm)
  • Chip baked at 90 degrees during 3 minutes

Total thickness of the layer : 100 nm
Thickness measured : No

PPA patterning

The hydrophilic treatment was too strong and the interesting region disappeared from the chip. Then this chip is entirely dedicated for training and we use it to compute the PPA etch rate of the oxygen plasma solution of the SPTS APS in CMi. Hence we patterned a serie of shapes useful for determining with a optical microscope the PPA thickness etched after a time t.
