Sample 1

This chip will contain some of the final gas sensors. The fabrication contains 3 main steps :

  • Pattern different shapes with the NanoFrazor and transfer these patterns into the SiO2 layer as best as possible.
  • 2D material transfer onto the best patterns.
  • Electrodes deposition.

Composition before patterning

- Substrate Layer 1
Material Si + SiO2 PPA
Thickness SiO2 : 200 nm 100 nm

Substrate preparation

  • Diced part of a wafer of Si + 200 nm SiO2
  • Cleaned in acetone during 5 minutes
  • Cleaned in isopropanole during 5 minutes
  • Dried with a N2 gun

PPA spin coating

Role of this layer : Layer to pattern with t-SPL technique.
Steps followed for the fabrication :

  • Spin coated PPA 5% at 2000 rpm (theoretical thickness : 100 nm)
  • Chip baked at 90 degrees during 3 minutes

Total thickness of the layer : 100 nm
Thickness measured : No

Pattern transfer

To transfer the patterns into the SiO2 layer, we use reactive ion etching with a CHF3/SF6 plasma solution.

Material Thickness of the layer Etch rate Time to etch all the layer
PPA 100 nm 110 nm/min 55 seconds
SiO2 200 nm 170 nm/min 70 seconds
CHF3/SF6 plasma solution characteristics

As the patterns begin at the top of the PPA layer, we must etch all the PPA to be sure all the patterns are transferred into the SiO2 layer. But to keep a margin to ensure we don't overetch (etch all the SiO2 layer), we choose a time of 1 minute.

Comments :

The plasma solution was supposed to etch more the SiO2 than the PPA, but the results show the inverse. The etch rate of the CHF3/SF6 plasma into the PPA was extracted from the Yiulya article (I don't know how to write it haha) and seems to be false for our PPA layer. Then, we have to look for another plasma solution if we want to increase the pattern amplitudes during the transfer into the SiO2 (maybe I can see that with the CMi staff ?).

Best result

The best transferred patterns are the triangular ones. The smaller the period, the better it is for our application, so we only detail for the smaller one in this section.

Real scale surface of the triangular 200 nm patterns

The amplitude of the pattern hasn't been amplified during the transfering into the SiO2 layer, hence the aspect ratio is very short. Indeed, the aspect ratio (amplitude / period) is around 0.06.
